# OctoAcme Project Management Docs

Welcome to the OctoAcme Project Management documentation hub! This README serves as the central entry point for all process documentation, providing a high-level overview of how OctoAcme delivers projects and offering quick navigation to every detailed guide. Whether you are a new contributor looking to get up to speed or a stakeholder seeking specific process guidance, you will find everything you need here.

## Overview

OctoAcme's project management processes are designed to promote clarity, consistent delivery, and stakeholder alignment across all initiatives. The workflow begins with a structured project initiation phase, where teams validate business needs, identify stakeholders, and produce a Project One-pager outlining goals, success metrics, risks, and resource requirements. Once a project passes the decision gate, detailed planning commences—work is broken into actionable backlog items, sprint lengths are defined, dependencies and risks are mapped out, and a clear Definition of Done is established for all deliverables. Throughout the project lifecycle, teams leverage templates and checklists to maintain transparency and ensure key tasks—like reviewing risk registers and running kickoff meetings—are systematically addressed.

OctoAcme emphasizes clear roles and responsibilities to support streamlined execution. Project Managers oversee delivery schedules, risk management, and cross-team coordination, while Product Managers own the product vision, define outcomes, and align priorities with customer value. Developers are responsible for designing and building solutions that meet defined acceptance criteria and quality expectations. Additional roles include QA/Testing personnel who handle validation and acceptance, and a variety of stakeholders who provide inputs, approvals, and feedback at designated milestones. These personas interact regularly—through daily standups, weekly syncs, demo/review sessions, and recurring stakeholder updates—to keep communication open, risks visible, and priorities aligned.

A core strength of OctoAcme's approach lies in its disciplined execution and tracking rhythm. Teams use GitHub Project boards or similar tools to visualize Work In Progress, progressing deliverables through standardized columns from backlog to QA and Done. Pull Request workflows are tightly defined: PRs should be small and linked to issues, must pass automated tests and linting prior to review, and require at least one approval before merging. Quality assurance is prioritized at multiple levels—unit, integration, and end-to-end tests are complemented by manual QA for critical features when required, and security scanning is embedded in the CI/CD pipeline. Key metrics and burndown charts are tracked, and risks are proactively managed and escalated using a three-tiered system (team, PM/Product Lead, and sponsor-level).

Continuous improvement is baked into the process, with retrospectives conducted after each sprint or milestone to capture learnings and generate actionable improvements. Action items from retrospectives are incorporated into backlogs, assigned clear owners and deadlines, and tracked during regular PM syncs. Communication templates—ranging from weekly status updates to incident summaries—ensure all stakeholders stay informed on progress, risks, and required decisions. By combining strong role clarity, proven quality practices, transparent workflows, and a culture of open communication and learning, OctoAcme enables teams to reliably deliver complex projects with clear alignment and accountability.
